# Day 7: The "Open to Work" Trap

## Morning Post (Educational)

### LinkedIn Post
Placing the green "Open to Work" badge on your LinkedIn profile photo does not attract recruiters. It repels them.

Many job seekers believe the badge signals availability and helps them get noticed. In reality, it can signal desperation. Recruiters are looking for in-demand talent, and high-value candidates are rarely positioned as desperate.

Recruiters don't search for "open to work" candidates. They search for specific skills, job titles, and expertise. If your profile headline says "Unemployed | Open to Work," you are missing the opportunity to show what you can actually do.

Remove the green badge. Optimize your headline to show your specific expertise and the value you deliver. Change "Actively seeking new opportunities" to "B2B Sales Representative | Helping SaaS companies scale outbound sales."

I've reviewed dozens of CVs and LinkedIn profiles, and those with focused, value-driven headlines receive far more inbound recruiter inquiries.

Position yourself as an active asset, not a job seeker waiting for a favor. You can use AI to identify the exact keywords recruiters search for in your industry.

## Afternoon Post (Story / Case Study)

### LinkedIn Post
She had the "Open to Work" badge for six months. Total recruiter messages: Zero.

She believed the Kenyan job market was completely dead and that recruiters were ignoring applications. The issue was her positioning. Her profile made her look like she was willing to do anything, which meant she specialized in nothing.

Recruiters don't hire generalists who want "any" job. They hire specialists who can solve a specific problem.

We removed the green badge. We rewrote her headline to focus on operations management and optimized her "About" section with keywords representing her actual project delivery metrics.

One of my clients got hired this week after we repositioned her from "actively seeking opportunities" to a focused operations expert.

Stop pleading for roles. Start positioning your expertise so recruiters seek you out.
