# Day 1: The Volume Trap

## Morning Post (Educational)

### LinkedIn Post
If you are sending the same CV to 50 different jobs, you are not actually applying. You are spamming.

Most job seekers believe job hunting is a numbers game. They spray and pray, sending out hundreds of applications, hoping someone will notice. The result? Dozens of applications, zero interview callbacks.

Recruiters don't read every CV. ATS (Applicant Tracking Systems) filter them, and recruiters scan the remaining ones in 6 seconds. If your CV doesn't directly map to the specific role's requirements, it gets rejected instantly.

Instead of applying to 10 jobs a day with one generic CV, apply to 2 jobs a day by tailoring your CV to match the job description. Highlight the exact achievements and metrics that align with what the company is actively looking for.

I've reviewed dozens of CVs, and the most common issue is a generic resume that tries to please everyone but ends up impressing no one.

Tailoring every application manually is exhausting. But there's a way to do it in minutes using AI without losing quality.

If this sounds familiar, I'll be teaching the complete workflow inside my upcoming AI Job Seeker Workshop. Seats are limited.
