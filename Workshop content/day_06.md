# Day 6: ChatGPT Is Not Your Writer

## Morning Post (Educational)

### LinkedIn Post
If your CV contains words like "delve," "testament," "leverage," or "revolutionized," recruiters know you copied and pasted from ChatGPT.

Many job seekers use generic prompts like "write a CV for a Sales Manager." ChatGPT spits out paragraphs of flowery corporate jargon that lacks substance. Recruiters read dozens of CVs daily and spot these AI patterns instantly.

Using AI without context and structure makes your application look generic and lazy. Recruiters want to read about your specific achievements, not a chatbot's idea of what a generic employee does.

Use AI as a structural analyzer and editor, not a writer. Prompt ChatGPT to analyze your own drafts, specify a direct, professional tone, and forbid it from using corporate buzzwords.

I've reviewed dozens of CVs, and the difference between a generic AI copy-paste and a resume refined with professional prompting is night and day.

You must retain your authentic voice while using AI to improve clarity and alignment.

If this sounds familiar, I'll be teaching the complete workflow inside my upcoming AI Job Seeker Workshop. Seats are limited.
