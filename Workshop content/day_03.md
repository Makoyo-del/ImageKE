# Day 3: The Certification Illusion

## Morning Post (Educational)

### LinkedIn Post
Having a list of 10 certificates on your CV won't get you hired if you can't demonstrate how you applied them.

Many job seekers collect certifications like trophies, believing that a longer list of credentials makes them more competitive. They spend months studying, but their inbox remains empty.

Recruiters don't hire certificates; they hire capabilities. A certificate proves you sat through a course and passed a test. It does not prove you can solve a real-world business problem.

Replace your list of certificates with a "Projects" or "Proof of Work" section. Detail a specific problem you solved, the tools you used (including AI), and the measurable result you achieved.

I've reviewed dozens of CVs, and the ones that showcase actual projects get prioritized over those with just a list of courses.

Shift your positioning from a student who knows things to a practitioner who builds things. You can use AI to frame your projects professionally.

If this sounds familiar, I'll be teaching the complete workflow inside my upcoming AI Job Seeker Workshop. Seats are limited.
