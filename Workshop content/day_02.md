# Day 2: The Keyword Myth

## Morning Post (Educational)

### LinkedIn Post
Jamming keywords into your CV is not "ATS optimization." It is keyword stuffing, and it will get you rejected.

Many job seekers copy and paste phrases from the job description into white text or random lists at the bottom of their resumes. They believe this tricks the system into giving them a high score.

Modern ATS are context-aware. They analyze where and how skills are mentioned. Even if you pass the system, a human recruiter will read the CV. When they see a list of terms with zero context, they click delete.

To optimize your CV correctly, place keywords within your work experience bullet points. Instead of just listing "Project Management," write: "Managed cross-functional projects to reduce delivery time by 15%."

I've reviewed dozens of CVs, and the ones filled with stuffed keywords almost always lack substance and get rejected during the initial human screen.

You must build a CV that satisfies both the software and the recruiter. You can use AI to do this without writing fake achievements.

## Afternoon Post (Story / Case Study)

### LinkedIn Post
"Can you just copy-paste these keywords into my CV so I can pass the system?"

Recruiters don't hire a glossary. They hire someone who can solve their specific business problems. A keyword without a metric to back it up is just noise.

One of my clients got hired this week after we focused on contextual alignment instead of basic keyword matching.
