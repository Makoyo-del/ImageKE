# Day 11: The "Why You?" Question

## Morning Post (Educational)

### LinkedIn Post
If your CV summary starts with "highly motivated, result-oriented professional," you have already lost the recruiter's interest.

Many job seekers fill their resume summaries with generic buzzwords that mean absolutely nothing to a hiring manager. They think it makes them sound professional, but it just sounds like white noise.

Recruiters scan right past these empty adjectives. They want to know: What is your target role? What is your core area of expertise? What is your biggest, most quantifiable business outcome?

Use the 3-Sentence Rule: Sentence 1: [Target Title] with [X] years of experience in [Core Field]. Sentence 2: Specialized in [Skill A] and [Skill B] to deliver [Result]. Sentence 3: Key achievement includes [Quantifiable Metric].

I've reviewed dozens of CVs, and summaries containing hard numbers get double the attention of summaries filled with buzzwords.

A summary should be a highlight reel of your facts, not a list of your personality traits. You can use AI to extract and structure your best achievements.

If this sounds familiar, I'll be teaching the complete workflow inside my upcoming AI Job Seeker Workshop. Seats are limited.
