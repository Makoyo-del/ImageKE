# Day 5: The Tailoring Bottleneck

## Morning Post (Educational)

### LinkedIn Post
If it takes you three hours to tailor your CV for a single job description, your process is broken.

Many job seekers spend half a day rewriting their career history for one application. They analyze every line, match every keyword manually, and end up too exhausted to apply for other opportunities.

You should not start from scratch for every job. The key is to have a modular master CV and a structured workflow that tells you exactly which parts of your experience to swap out.

Create a master CV with all your career details. When applying, use AI to compare your master CV with the target job and extract only the bullet points that match their criteria.

I've reviewed dozens of CVs, and the most successful candidates are those who use a modular system to target roles quickly without sacrificing quality.

Tailoring is about strategic alignment, not starting over. You can use AI to do this in under 10 minutes.

If this sounds familiar, I'll be teaching the complete workflow inside my upcoming AI Job Seeker Workshop. Seats are limited.
