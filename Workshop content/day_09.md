# Day 9: The Recruiter's Inbox

## Morning Post (Educational)

### LinkedIn Post
When you click "Apply Now," your CV goes into a digital black hole unless it ranks high in the recruiter's system.

Many job seekers assume a human reads their resume immediately. In reality, Applicant Tracking Systems parse and rank candidates based on match percentage. If your CV ranks below a certain threshold, the recruiter may never even open your file.

Bypassing the system filter is not about luck. It is about aligning your CV with the exact search queries the recruiter uses to find candidates.

Review the job description to find the primary technical and functional skills they need. Make sure these are written exactly as requested in your experience section, not hidden in a generic list.

I've reviewed dozens of CVs, and I frequently see highly qualified candidates get filtered out simply because of a terminology mismatch.

You must learn to translate your experience into the language the recruiter's system expects. You can use AI to do this translation in seconds.

If this sounds familiar, I'll be teaching the complete workflow inside my upcoming AI Job Seeker Workshop. Seats are limited.
