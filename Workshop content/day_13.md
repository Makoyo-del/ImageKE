## Morning Post (Educational)

### LinkedIn Post
There is a massive difference between knowing ChatGPT exists and knowing how to use it to land a job.

Many job seekers copy-paste basic prompts they find on social media. They get a generic resume, submit it, and wonder why they are still getting automated rejection emails.

Generic prompts produce generic outputs. To stand out, you need a structured, context-rich workflow that translates your actual experience into high-impact, recruiter-ready achievements.

When using AI, always: (1) Assign a professional persona to the AI, (2) Feed it the exact job requirements as source data, (3) Input your raw metrics, and (4) Direct it to write in a simple, direct tone with no buzzwords.

I've reviewed dozens of CVs, and the resumes built with structured, context-based prompts consistently outperform those built with basic, one-line prompts.

Stop using basic prompts that make you sound like a robot. Learn to prompt like a professional.

If this sounds familiar, I'll be teaching the complete workflow inside my upcoming AI Job Seeker Workshop. Seats are limited.
