# Day 4: Recruiter Behavior & The 6-Second Scan

## Morning Post (Educational)

### LinkedIn Post
Recruiters do not read your CV. They scan it.

Many job seekers write long, dense paragraphs in their profile summary, thinking the recruiter will read every word. They hide their best achievements in the middle of a wall of text.

The 6-second scan is a real recruiter habit. In those few seconds, they only look at: job titles, company names, dates of employment, and the top-fold summary. If your layout is cluttered or hard to read, they move to the next candidate.

Keep your profile summary under three sentences. Use bullet points instead of paragraphs for achievements. Use standard headings (like "Professional Experience" instead of "Where I've Been") so the recruiter's eye knows where to go.

I've reviewed dozens of CVs, and the ones with clean formatting and clear visual hierarchy always get parsed better by both ATS software and human recruiters.

You don't need a fancy design; you need a structured layout that highlights your value instantly. You can use AI to write summary sections that hook the recruiter.

## Afternoon Post (Story / Case Study)

### LinkedIn Post
We changed the first three inches of her CV, and she got an interview.

She was highly experienced in project management, but her CV was a wall of text. Her achievements were buried under descriptions of company history.

Recruiters are busy. If they have to search for your value, they assume it does not exist. Your best achievements must be visible at a glance.

We restructured the top fold of her CV. We removed the corporate jargon and put three clear, metric-driven achievements right below her contact details.

One of my clients got hired this week after we focused on optimizing the layout and readability of her CV.

You don't need to rewrite your entire career history; you just need to position it where recruiters can see it.
